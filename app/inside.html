8 AI Tools That Give Designers Amazing New Capabilities

Author: Bohdan Popravko

26 Feb 2024

AI tools are developing at a crazy pace. And this scares many designers. But what if we perceive artificial intelligence not as a potential competitor and rival, but as an ally and use its power to our advantage?

Technology is constantly evolving, and this directly affects design. The future of artificial intelligence is still unclear, but one thing is for sure - innovation is inevitable. So instead of worrying about whether we'd be out of work, I decided to use the power of AI to my advantage.

AI has revolutionized various industries, and the world of design is no exception.

Screensaver created using generative artificial intelligence

We find ourselves on the cusp of radical change that challenges traditional ideas about what creativity is. For centuries, creativity has been considered an innate human trait—the divine spark that sets us apart from machines. But now that artificial intelligence is firmly established in our lives, we must reconsider the boundaries of creative expression.

We see AI automating tasks, making predictions, and even creating works of art. But how can it help us build a brand?

Today we will look at 8 tools designed specifically for this:

1. Kittl

Kittl: Unleash your creativity.

Kittl is an AI-powered tool that allows even users with no graphic design experience to create professional-looking vector illustrations, icons, logos, text effects, and more. This is a great starting point for working on your corporate identity and visual experiments.

Not only can you generate images based on text prompts, but you can also edit them on the spot. It's a kind of collaboration—the AI creates the image, and you use your creativity to refine it.

Kittl: Unleash your creativity.

Recently I needed to develop branding for a Greek restaurant. I used Chat GPT to help narrow down possible names and logo options. After settling on "Olympian Eatery" and choosing an olive branch inspired design, I moved on to creating a mood board.

Kittl: Unleash your creativity.

Instead of spending hours searching for references on Pinterest, I took a more efficient approach: the Kittl image generator. A few queries on Greek themes, and the mood board is ready. After that, I converted the images into vector format directly in Kittl.

Not all AI tools are capable of vectorizing images, but Kittl provides this opportunity. This makes the design process more flexible and Kittl an exceptionally functional and valuable application for designers.

Conclusion

The impact of artificial intelligence extends beyond the generation of logos and visual elements. It now extends to every aspect of your brand. You can create videos, product photo shoots, and even fully functional websites from a single text query.

Of course, the result is not ideal yet. We still need creative skills to make it perfect. However, AI has significantly narrowed the gap between idea and implementation, opening up greater opportunities for experimentation and allowing designers to bring their ideas to life faster and more efficiently.

With AI as an ally, our creative journey becomes not only faster, but also richer. The future looks promising and I am excited and optimistic about the use of AI in design.
